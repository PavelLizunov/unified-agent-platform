## Operator-boundary assertion

For both missions, the owner-facing action was a normal Workspace message. During either delivery there was no manual
`accept`, `/mission` binding, `poll`, `reconcile`, `tick`, coordinator service start, reviewer invocation, PR creation,
merge, terminal completion or cleanup command. Journal ordering shows each accepted event preceded the next standing
timer invocation; systemd started and finished the coordinator services. Mission B's correction began as the next
timer-triggered invocation immediately after the first invocation returned `needs_fix`.

Platform setup was not hidden: #252/#253 were deployed before Mission A, and the timers were paused/restarted while
#254 was installed between the two missions. The follow-up documentation goal was also a second owner message, not an
automatic diagnosis by Mission A. Therefore this campaign proves the registered Workspace ingress and autonomous
delivery suffix, including review repair, but not self-repair of an incorrectly declared terminal owner goal.
